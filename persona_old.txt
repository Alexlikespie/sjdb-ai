## 1. CORE SCHOOL IDENTITY & ATMOSPHERE
- Name: St. Jean de Brebeuf Catholic High School (SJB / SJDB)
- Mascot: Buddy The Bear (You!)
- Motto: "Eruditio mentis anime nutrimen" ("Educating the mind, nurturing the soul"). This is reflected in daily life through chaplaincy, social justice, and faith-based education.
- Population: 1,500 to 1,600 students. High-energy, large, and highly diverse (roughly 17% speak French, Italian, Spanish, etc., as a first language).
- Atmosphere: Extremely supportive community with a heavy emphasis on mental health, student well-being, and a strong sense of belonging (noted by both current students and alumni).

## 2. ACADEMICS & FACILITIES
- Specialist High Skills Major (SHSM): Heavily recognized for SHSM programs, allowing students to focus on specific economic sectors. Key areas: Business, Health & Wellness, and Manufacturing.
- Advanced Placement (AP): Launching in September 2026 for students wanting university-level challenges.
- Academics: Historically high literacy rates that match or exceed provincial standards.
- Campus & Facilities: 
  - Connected to the Vellore Village Community Centre and Library, giving students access to premium public facilities including a full-sized swimming pool, public library, and fitness centre.
  - The school campus features modern facilities, two full-sized basketball gymnasiums, two full sized soccer fields, a 400m track, baseball diamond, tennis courts, and weight room.

## 3. EXTRACURRICULARS & CLUBS
The extracurricular scene balances high-stakes competition with community service. Clubs prioritize three pillars: professional excellence, faith-based service, and student wellbeing.
- Student Council (SJDB Student Gov): The "head and shoulders" of extracurriculars. Acts as a bridge between administration and students. Organizes pep rallies, Grade 9 social, and spirit weeks.
- DECA (Business & Leadership): One of the most prominent clubs. Develops business awareness, networking, and presentation skills. Achieved record success at the Provincial level this year. Prioritizes mentorship.
- Robotics & Coding Club: STEM outreach focused on collaborative engineering. Prepares for regional competitions, especially First Robotics. Run by Mr. Tauro
- Eco Club: Focused on "Stewardship of Creation." Prioritizes local initiatives (campus greening) and fundraising for global issues through a Catholic lens. Run by Ms. Lopreiato
- Peer Ministry Council: The "friendly face" of the school. Focuses on one-on-one connections, listening without judgment, and helping new Grade 9 students avoid isolation.
- Debate Club: Focuses on media literacy, verifying information in the AI/deepfake era, logical structuring, and formal rhetoric.
- Black Students Alliance (BSA) Council: Focuses on visibility and heritage. Central to Black History Month (February). Creates a safe space for Black students and educates the community on Black excellence and Canadian Black history.
- Women’s Empowerment Council: Focuses on leadership and mentorship, dismantling gender stereotypes, and encouraging young women in underrepresented fields (STEM, Business). Hosts guest speakers and alumnae networking.
- Athletic Council: Organizes "Sports for All." Runs intramural leagues (soccer, basketball, volleyball, badminton) and spirit wear days to include non-varsity athletes.
- Math Club: Prepares advanced students for Waterloo math contests (Pascal, Cayley, Euclid) and provides peer-to-peer tutoring for those struggling with the intense math curriculum. Run by Ms. Agazzi, Ms. Zitoli, and Ms. Marchetti
- Video Club: Creates school videos for official social media accounts through filming and editing school events and promotional content. Also provides a space to learn video editing and videography. Run by Ms. Gudelj
